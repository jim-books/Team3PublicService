# the Story of Seed

![SEED logo](docs/assets/seed-logo.png)

**SEED - School Education Evaluation and Development**

*Planting the future of education.*

## Overview

**SEED** is an open, data-driven platform for making education-resource decisions more accessible, evidence-based, and actionable.

We were inspired by the challenge identified in Thailand's education system: resources are often allocated through broad, traditional methods that do not fully reflect the real conditions of individual schools. The result is chronic misallocation, widening inequality, and weaker learning environments for students who already face the greatest barriers.

SEED responds to that problem by turning fragmented school data into practical decision support. Instead of asking policymakers, school leaders, and researchers to work from disconnected spreadsheets or incomplete reports, SEED helps them understand where support is most needed, why those gaps exist, and what interventions could make the greatest difference.

## The Problem

Improving education for all starts with better information.

Today, many public-service decisions in education are still slowed down by:

- inconsistent school reporting
- limited visibility into school-level conditions
- weak links between collected data and budget decisions
- high administrative burden on schools and government offices
- limited public-service research access to usable, structured datasets

When data is hard to collect, hard to interpret, and hard to act on, even well-intentioned funding can miss the schools that need help most.

## Our Core Idea

SEED makes data-driven decision-making in education more accessible and effective.

The platform combines school evaluation, reporting workflows, and decision tools in one system so stakeholders can move from **data collection** to **diagnosis** to **resource allocation** more quickly and more confidently.

It is aligned with the direction outlined in [`docs/school-index-brief.md`](docs/school-index-brief.md): use contextual school data, quality indicators, and evidence-based analysis to support more equitable allocation of public resources.

## Three Core Features

### 1. Decision Dashboard for Government and NGOs

SEED provides a data dashboard with natural-language querying so decision-makers can ask practical questions in plain English, such as:

- Which schools show the highest infrastructure gaps?
- Where should limited funding be prioritized first?
- Which districts combine high need with low current support?

This lowers the barrier to evidence-based policymaking for ministries, local governments, foundations, and NGOs that need faster answers without relying on technical analysts for every question.

### 2. Easier Reporting and School Administration

SEED helps schools and public administrators collect and structure data more efficiently.

Instead of spending excessive time on fragmented reporting processes, users can work with lightweight AI-assisted tools, including concepts such as an **AI classroom quality inspector**, to document school conditions, summarize findings, and prepare usable reports. This reduces administrative cost and frees educators and administrators to focus on education itself.

### 3. Research Dataset API and Advanced Analytics

SEED also supports researchers, policy analysts, and mission-driven organizations that want to study how public-service resources can be allocated more effectively.

Through a dataset API and premium analytics layer, researchers can access richer structured data for analysis on school quality, inequality, and intervention effectiveness. This creates a pathway for continuous learning, better policy design, and a sustainable model for maintaining high-value analytical services.

## Who SEED Serves

- **Government decision-makers** who need clearer evidence for budget allocation
- **NGOs and education foundations** looking to target interventions more effectively
- **School administrators** who need simpler reporting workflows
- **Researchers and policy analysts** studying educational equity and public-service optimization
- **Partners and sponsors** in education, philanthropy, and social-impact sectors who want to support a credible, data-backed platform

## Why It Matters

SEED is not just a dashboard. It is a public-service infrastructure concept.

By helping stakeholders see school conditions more clearly and act on that evidence more quickly, SEED can:

- improve the fairness of resource allocation
- reduce wasted time and reporting friction
- support long-term accountability for under-resourced schools
- help close the quality gap across different communities
- create better learning environments for students who need them most

## Partners, Sponsors, and Sustainability

SEED is designed to align with partners and sponsors across the education ecosystem, including NGOs, education-sector organizations, and mission-aligned companies.

This creates value on two levels:

1. **Public impact:** better evidence for decisions that affect schools and students
2. **Strategic collaboration:** a focused niche audience of decision-makers, operators, and researchers in education

The open platform can serve broad public-good use cases, while advanced analytics, premium datasets, and tailored partner collaborations can support long-term sustainability.

- **AI for Public Services**
- **education equity**
- **evidence-based policymaking**
- **data infrastructure for social impact**

For this hackathon, SEED is our way of showing how AI can support better public-service decisions, not by replacing people, but by making complex education data more usable for the people responsible for acting on it.
