Story to evidence to action
The Problem

Education funding still struggles to see schools as they really are.

Too often, school conditions are buried in fragmented reports, broad averages, and slow manual processes. The result is a gap between where support is sent and where need is greatest.

SEED reframes education allocation as a visibility problem. When school data becomes structured, contextual, and interpretable, better public decisions become possible.

Why current systems break down

  • School reporting is inconsistent and slow.
  • Decision-makers cannot compare local context clearly.
  • Evidence often arrives too late to shape action.
How SEED Works

Three connected capabilities turn data into fairer decisions.

SEED combines decision support, AI-assisted reporting, and research-ready data access in one clear system. The page should feel like a real product, not just a concept summary.

01

Decision dashboard for governments and NGOs

Explore school conditions, compare districts, and identify where limited support can create the greatest educational impact.

02

AI-assisted reporting for schools

Help administrators gather, structure, and summarize school information with less manual burden and more usable output.

03

Research-facing dataset and analytics layer

Give policy analysts and education researchers access to cleaner, richer evidence for long-term equity work.

AI In The Loop

AI matters here because it lowers the barrier from data to action.

The strongest demo moment is not "there is AI somewhere in the stack." It is showing that a real user can ask a practical question and receive a clearer next step.

Example live demo moment

Natural language query

Which schools show the highest infrastructure gaps and should be prioritized first for support?

SEED responds with a structured recommendation, not just raw data:

  • rank the highest-need schools
  • explain the context behind the ranking
  • highlight where funding could have the strongest effect

Why this matters

Fairer allocation Support can move toward actual school conditions instead of broad assumptions.
Lower reporting friction Schools spend less time assembling fragmented evidence and more time acting on it.
Better public accountability Decision-makers can explain not only what they fund, but why they fund it.
